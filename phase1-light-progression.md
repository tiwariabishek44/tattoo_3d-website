# Phase 1 — The Light Progression (Videos 01–10)

> **Phase goal:** Learn to make a simple object feel *premium with light alone.*
> A sphere is the purest test surface in 3D — it shows you everything light does with
> zero distraction. Master this and you've earned the camera (Phase 2).
>
> **Hard constraints for ALL 10 (the "Don't add yet" rule):**
> - 🚫 **No camera moves** — near-static. Camera is Phase 2.
> - 🚫 **No fancy materials** — matte / simple only. Chrome & titanium are Phase 3.
> - 🚫 **No extra objects, atmosphere, or story.** One object, one idea per video.
>

## Video 01 — Key Light, Upper-Left
- **Focus:** The single most important setup in all of lighting — one motivated key from the upper-left.
- **Setup:** Matte sphere, dark seamless void, one soft key light upper-left. Near-static frame.
- **Observe & learn:** The **shadow terminator** — the gradient where lit wraps to dark. This single gradient is where "premium" is won or lost. Watch the highlight, the falloff, the contact shadow.
- **Done when:** You can point to the terminator and say "*that* gradient is why it feels real."

## Video 02 — Key Light, Side (90°)
- **Focus:** Move the same key to a true side angle. Half the sphere falls into shadow.
- **Observe & learn:** How **side light sculpts form** — maximum sense of volume and roundness. Notice how much more "3D" it reads than front-lit.
- **Done when:** You understand that *where* the light sits changes the entire sense of form, with nothing else touched.

## Video 03 — Backlight / Rim Only
- **Focus:** Light only from behind — no front fill. The object goes near-silhouette with a glowing edge.
- **Observe & learn:** **Rim/edge light = separation.** This thin line lifts an object off its background. It's the secret ingredient in almost every premium product shot.
- **Done when:** You can see how a single rim turns a flat shape into something that floats in space.

## Video 04 — Key + Rim Together
- **Focus:** Combine 01 and 03 — soft key upper-left *plus* a rim from behind.
- **Observe & learn:** **Form AND separation at the same time.** This is the workhorse setup you'll reuse for the rest of your career. Balance them — rim should support, not overpower.
- **Done when:** The sphere has clear volume *and* clean separation, and you balanced the two by eye.

## Video 05 — Swap to a Cube
- **Focus:** Same key+rim setup, but a **cube**. Light now hits flat planes and hard edges.
- **Observe & learn:** How light reads as **flat tonal blocks on planes** and crisp highlights on hard edges — totally different from a curve's smooth gradient.
- **Done when:** You can predict how a plane will tone *before* you render it.

## Video 06 — Rounded Form
- **Focus:** A rounded-edge form (squircle / rounded cube) — the bridge between sphere and cube.
- **Observe & learn:** The **soft edge** — how a chamfer or fillet catches a bright, thin highlight. This is *the* shape language of premium product design (every Apple device lives here).
- **Done when:** You see why rounded edges read as "expensive" — they give light a clean line to grab.

## Video 07 — Warm vs Cool
- **Focus:** Same form, shift the **color temperature** of the key — one warm version, one cool.
- **Observe & learn:** Temperature is **emotion**, not decoration. Warm = inviting/luxe; cool = clinical/premium-tech. Same geometry, opposite feeling.
- **Done when:** You can set a mood with temperature alone, deliberately.

## Video 08 — Hard vs Soft Shadow
- **Focus:** Hold everything; change only the **source size**. Small source = hard sharp shadow; large source = soft gradient shadow.
- **Observe & learn:** **Shadow quality is a dial.** Soft = premium, gentle, modern. Hard = dramatic, tense, graphic. The source size controls it.
- **Done when:** You can choose a shadow's hardness on purpose and explain the mood it creates.

## Video 09 — Low-Key / Chiaroscuro
- **Focus:** Mostly darkness, one sliver of light. Drama through **restraint**.
- **Observe & learn:** How *removing* light creates tension and presence. Most of the frame is black; the object emerges. This is the most "cinematic" lighting and the hardest to balance.
- **Done when:** The object feels powerful and mysterious with most of it hidden — and it still reads clearly.

## Video 10 — High-Key / Clean Premium (Capstone)
- **Focus:** The opposite of 09 — bright, soft, minimal shadow. The clean, floating, "Apple-page" product look.
- **Observe & learn:** Achieving *premium* through **cleanliness and control**, not drama. Soft even light, gentle contact shadow, generous negative space. This is the capstone — it demands everything from 01–08 working together.
- **Done when:** A simple object looks like it belongs on a flagship product page — clean, calm, expensive — and you know every light decision that got it there.

## Phase 1 Graduation Criteria

You're ready for Phase 2 (Camera) when **all** are true:
- [ ] You can place a key light to get any sense of form you want, on purpose.
- [ ] You can add a rim for separation without overpowering the key.
- [ ] You can read a shadow terminator and judge a render by it.
- [ ] You can set mood with **temperature** and **shadow hardness** deliberately.
- [ ] You can hit both a **dramatic low-key** and a **clean high-key** look on command.
- [ ] For any clip, you can name the *one* light decision that made it feel premium.

> When you can do this with a static object, adding a camera move (Phase 2) becomes
> a joy instead of a crutch. Light first. Always.
