## How to use this doc

1. Work the phases **in order**. Don't skip ahead — each one builds the muscle the next needs.
2. Obey the **"Don't add yet"** column. It's discipline, not restriction — it stops you over-reaching before the basics are locked.
3. **Log every video** (template below). The log is where 50 clips become skill.
4. A phase is done only when its **"Done when…"** check genuinely passes — not when you've hit the video count.
5. **Scrub-test** every clip (drag slowly, frame by frame). Playback lies; scrub tells the truth.

## 1. The Phase Playbook

| Phase | The ONE thing to master | Don't add yet | Done when… |
|-------|------------------------|---------------|------------|
| **01–10 · Geometry & Light** | single light source, mood, soft shadow on simple shapes | camera moves, fancy materials | a sphere/cube feels *premium* with zero motion |
| **11–25 · Camera & Perspective** | one clean move per clip (push / orbit / C-S arc) | complex materials, assembly | a C-curve arc feels smooth when scrubbed |
| **26–40 · Color & Materials** | chrome, titanium, matte + mid-motion light shifts | multi-part assembly | a surface reads as genuinely *expensive* |
| **41–50 · Concepts** | assembly / exploded / multi-part stories | nothing — now combine everything | it survives a slow scrub without morphing |

### Phase detail

**01–10 · Geometry & Light** — One object, near-static camera. Master mood with a single motivated key, soft shadow, and reflections. Goal: prove you can make *nothing happen* and still feel premium. Lighting is 80% of "expensive."

**11–25 · Camera & Perspective** — Introduce ONE deliberate camera move per clip. Practice C/S-curve arcs over static spins. This is the longest phase on purpose — camera *is* the storyteller for scroll work.

**26–40 · Color & Materials** — Premium material craft (brushed titanium, liquid chrome, matte, glass) and shifting light color mid-motion. Learn how light *reveals* material truth.

**41–50 · Concepts** — Full immersive scenes: abstract assembly, exploded views, modular reveals. Use the **Chunked Continuity Strategy** (Science doc §11) — these are morph-prone, so build them as deterministic beats with last-frame handoffs.

## 2. Prompt-Vocabulary Cheat Sheet

The real transferable skill — the *words* that map to Veo's training captions. Reach for these deliberately, not randomly.

**Camera**
`dolly / push in` · `crane / boom` · `orbit` · `macro` · `tracking` · `C-curve arc` · `S-curve arc` · `low-angle` · `three-quarter hero angle`

**Depth**
`shallow depth of field` · `bokeh` · `foreground occlusion` · `parallax layers` · `atmospheric perspective`

**Atmosphere**
`volumetric light` · `god rays` · `haze` · `floating particles` · `dust motes`

**Light**
`rim light` · `soft key` · `three-point studio` · `motivated light` · `chiaroscuro` · `color temperature shift`

**Color / Material**
`brushed titanium` · `liquid chrome` · `matte finish` · `glass` · `cool steel-blue` · `warm amber accent` · `metal grain`

**Mood / Pacing**
`slow motion` · `ease-in-out` · `monumental scale` · `weightless float` · `deliberate reveal` · `single continuous shot, no cuts`

**Style DNA** (concrete, not buzzwords)
`shot on 35mm` · `natural film grain` · `anamorphic lens flare` · `cinematic realism` · `premium product shot`

> For how to *assemble* these into a prompt per input mode (text-only, reference-image, start+end-frame), use [prompt-architecture.md](prompt-architecture.md). This sheet is the word bank; that doc is the grammar.

## 3. Per-Video Log Template

Copy this block for every video. This is the highest-leverage habit in the whole curriculum.

```
### Video 07 — Phase: Geometry & Light
Tool:        Veo / Flow / Seedance / Higgsfield
Contract:    text→video / ref→image / frames→video
Prompt:      <paste exact prompt>
✅ Worked:    rim light sold the metal
❌ Failed:    shadow flickered when scrubbed
→ Next:      lock the light, slow the arc 20%
Scrub test:  PASS / FAIL
```
